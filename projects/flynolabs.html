FlynoLabs — Experimental Drone Design and Prototyping

2023-01-15

FlynoLabs is where bold ideas meet cutting-edge prototyping. This R&D arm of Flynoceros serves as a playground for drone enthusiasts, engineers, and dreamers alike. We're not afraid to push boundaries or experiment with the unconventional—because that's where the magic happens.

Y-Knot

The Y-Knot platform was built for experimentation and adaptability, breaking away from traditional quadcopter designs. By rethinking configurations like Y4s and V-tails, we crafted a versatile design centered around a robust back beam, allowing for limitless customization.

One of the key innovations is integrated feet, addressing a common issue with Y4 designs: stability during landing. These feet protect inverted motors and propellers while serving as connection points for modular accessories.

The Y-Knot also explores vertical arm configurations and sandwiched motor plates. Using perpendicular carbon fiber plates secured with three connection points, we minimized racking while maintaining strength and durability. This sturdy design has proven successful, offering exciting opportunities for larger drone applications.

RatKwad

The RatKwad reimagines FPV drone design by challenging the traditional placement of the camera and battery. Inspired by the iconic view over the dash of old-school rat rods, the RatKwad places the FPV camera above the drone's canopy, offering pilots a unique perspective that includes a glimpse of their own drone in flight. This design isn't just fun—it also prioritizes practicality.

By centering the battery within the drone, the RatKwad solves the age-old dilemma of weight distribution. Placing the mass at the center reduces the moment of inertia, improves stability, and enhances battery efficiency during turns. With a design that mirrors the layout of a classic rat rod, the motors and propellers mimic the wheel drive system, the battery acts as the engine, and the electronics are housed securely in the "cab."

The result? A durable, balanced drone with a distinctive style and an exhilarating flight experience. Building and flying the RatKwad isn't just a hobby—it's pure fun.

Delivery Drone

The Delivery Drone was a passion project that combined functionality with vintage-inspired aesthetics. Born from the question, "What if a drone had a practical use?" the goal was to create a drone capable of delivering a simple, lightweight item—like a can of soda—while maintaining a unique, stylish design.

Drawing inspiration from old milk trucks, the drone features a cab-like front section for the electronics and battery, reminiscent of the RatKwad, and a van-style rear compartment for the payload. The rear section opens and closes, allowing for secure transport of the shipment. Balancing practicality and performance, the drone underwent three design iterations to achieve a sleek, functional design that performs beautifully while turning heads.

This project was a delightful exploration of how drones can be both useful and visually captivating, delivering not just goods but a sense of nostalgia and creativity.
